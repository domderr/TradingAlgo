Weekly update - Monday, June 29, 2026

TradingAlgo Mosaic: selective strength into the new week

The new week opens with a highly selective picture for the TradingAlgo Mosaic portfolios. Dispersion remains high across regions and indices: several portfolios delivered clear relative strength versus their benchmarks, while others were held back by weaker local markets or less favorable sector rotation.

6 Markets with clear relative strength: DJ30, USA100, Nasdaq100, Mexico30, Germany30 and S.Africa30.
+4.36 pp Mexico30 relative spread versus benchmark, the strongest weekly gap in the dashboard.
20% S.Korea30 current hedge, the highest hedge level after a difficult week for the local market.

The strongest contribution came from the US portfolios. DJ30 gained 2.52% versus a 0.73% benchmark move, confirming solid stock selection quality. The rotation into WMT and out of CVX points to a more defensive and resilient portfolio mix. USA100 also performed well, rising 1.28% while its benchmark declined 1.67%. The entry of GEV in place of SLB strengthens a basket already exposed to names such as MU, CAT, FDX and GOOGL.

The Nasdaq100 result was more modest in absolute terms, with the portfolio up 0.13%, but still materially ahead of a benchmark down 3.17%. Rotation was more active here, with MRVL and RKLB entering the selection while WBD and ROST exited. The hedge increased from 10% to 12%, signalling a slightly more cautious stance in a still volatile market.

In North America outside the US, Canada50 remained under pressure at -0.94%, below a benchmark at -0.43%. The internal change brings TD.TO into the portfolio and removes CM.TO, while the hedge edges up from 14% to 15%. Mexico30 was much stronger, advancing 2.03% against a benchmark decline of 2.33%. The entry of LIVEPOLC-1.MX in place of VOLARA.MX accompanies one of the best relative performances of the week.

Europe was more mixed and generally weaker. Europe50 fell 3.68%, underperforming a benchmark down 1.31%, even as the hedge eased from 10% to 9%. UK30 also struggled, losing 3.13% while its benchmark rose 0.92%. France40 and Spain40 were also negative, down 2.77% and 2.52% respectively.

The clear European exception was Germany30, which gained 1.87% versus a benchmark decline of 1.08%. The current selection, including ENR.DE, RWE.DE, IFX.DE, BAYN.DE and DHL.DE, delivered a meaningful weekly spread despite a slightly lower hedge, from 13% to 12%. Italy30 remained weak at -2.54%, broadly in line with its benchmark at -2.72%.

In Asia-Pacific, conditions remain complex. Japan50 declined 2.78%, slightly ahead of a benchmark down 2.98%. S.Korea30 posted the most difficult weekly result, falling 11.91% against a benchmark decline of 6.48%. The hedge increased sharply from 16% to 20%, while 055550.KS entered and 006400.KS exited, confirming a more defensive adjustment after the deterioration in the Korean market.

Australia50 closed at -2.19%, almost aligned with its benchmark at -2.21%, with the hedge moving up from 16% to 17%. In South Africa, S.Africa30 lost 2.54% but outperformed a benchmark down 5.35%. The entry of AGL.JO in place of ANG.JO helped maintain better relative resilience, while the hedge decreased from 15% to 14%.

Overall, the weekly message is clear: market dispersion remains elevated, portfolio rotation continues to matter, and hedge management remains a central part of navigating unstable market conditions. TradingAlgo Mosaic showed its strongest relative profile in DJ30, USA100, Nasdaq100, Mexico30, Germany30 and S.Africa30, while Europe, the UK and South Korea require a more cautious read into the new week.
